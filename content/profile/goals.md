# Career Goals

## Target identity
**Software Engineer with strong AI/ML capability**, OR **AI Engineer with strong software/systems fundamentals.** Hybrid role — not pure research, not pure frontend. Backend + cloud + LLM/RAG product work is the sweet spot.

Rationale: already senior on the software/cloud/security side. Adding AI depth turns an existing strength into a differentiated profile rather than starting a new career from zero.

## Primary target: Saudi Arabia (2026)
- Vision 2030 is pouring money into AI, cloud, and digital infrastructure. Tax-free comp.
- Companies on the radar: NEOM, Saudi Aramco Digital, stc, Delivery Hero MENA (Talabat), AWS/SAP/Oracle Riyadh offices, Saudi banking tech, HUMAIN.
- Existing UAE employment history + Gulf familiarity is an advantage.
- Active prep tracker: [/saudi-arabia](/saudi-arabia).

## Secondary target: Germany (later)
- Uses the **same profile data** — same skills, same projects, same experience.
- What changes: CV format (Europass-style), cover letter norms, visa route (EU Blue Card — easier with CS degree + senior role + qualifying salary), English-speaking Berlin/Munich tech scene.
- German language not required for most international tech roles but A1–A2 helps socially and for permanent residency later.

## Non-negotiables / preferences
- **Relocation to Saudi Arabia is the top priority.** Must come with family support: spouse + infant dependent visas, housing allowance, family health insurance, schooling allowance down the line.
- Remote-from-Bangladesh Saudi roles are an acceptable **fallback** if the right on-site offer doesn't land in the Oct/Nov 2026 wave — but only as a bridge, not the end state.
- AI/backend hybrid, not legacy-only maintenance.
- Team that takes security seriously.
- Self-taught dev by preference — no certifications today. Willing to pick up AWS / Kubernetes / security certs **only if** they unblock a specific role (not as general resume padding).

## Compensation

**Current:** ~$3,000 USD/month base + occasional bonus (significantly underpaid relative to market for the role and experience — this is leverage, not a ceiling).

**Saudi Arabia target** (all tax-free, most with housing + family health + schooling + annual flights home on top):
- **Floor (walk-away):** SAR 22,000/month (~$5,900). Anything below this isn't worth the move with a new baby.
- **Realistic target:** SAR 30,000–38,000/month (~$8,000–$10,100). This is where a Lead SWE with 9 yrs, 6 yrs AWS, security + applied-AI should land at a Vision-2030 company or multinational.
- **Stretch:** SAR 42,000–50,000/month (~$11,200–$13,300). Possible at NEOM, Aramco Digital, or a well-funded Riyadh multinational if the AI capstone and portfolio land well.

Add on top: housing allowance (commonly 25% of base), family medical, annual return flights, end-of-service gratuity, sometimes schooling allowance once the child is of age.

**Germany target** (gross, pre-tax):
- **EU Blue Card minimum (2026, IT/STEM shortage):** ~€48,300/year gross — the legal floor for Blue Card eligibility. Not a target, just a constraint.
- **Realistic target for Senior/Lead SWE in Berlin / Munich:** €80,000–105,000/year gross base. With stock/bonus at better companies, total comp €90k–120k.
- **Stretch:** €110k–130k gross at FAANG Munich, adtech, or a strong Series-C+ startup.

Important: Germany is high tax (~35–42% effective). Saudi take-home will almost always beat Germany take-home at the same headline number. That's the main reason Saudi is the primary target first.

## Explicit non-goals
- **Arabic language study.** Saudi tech hiring at the senior level runs in English; Arabic is not a gate for the target companies. Not a weakness to fix — a conscious allocation of prep time toward ML depth and system design instead.

## Current constraint
- Main skill gap is deep ML/DL theory. Strong on applied AI (RAG, LangChain, LLM APIs, vector DBs) but needs to be conversational on fundamentals (backprop, transformers, loss functions, evaluation) for interviews. That's what the 30-week tracker is for.
